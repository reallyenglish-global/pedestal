EZ to Talk is a set of online English speaking lessons supporting the Practical English 6 e-learning course.

EZ to Talk consists of preparation lessons (e-learning), live lessons (speaking lessons) and follow-up les sons (e-learning). Have a look at the steps below to find out how to begin studying.

1 - Find the lesson you need, and download the PDF teaching materials.

Make a note of the lesson title and whether it is Grammar, Reading or Listening.

2 - Log in to your Reallyenglish Mypage and launch Practical English 6

Click the ‘Start the lesson’ button to launch Practical English 6.

3 - Search for the same lesson in Practical English 6.

Click the tab for Grammar, Reading or Listening, and search for the lesson title in either Japanese or Eng lish.

4 - Book an English speaking lesson on EZ ToTalk

Select the date and time of your choice, and check the ‘ticket use’ using the curriculum search.

5 - Select the tutor and the time you would like your lesson

Click ‘Open’ for a reservation confirmation screen.

6 - Confirm your reservation

Select the ticket always (???). Please put the check in the corresponding of if you can not o er lessons in 2 QQEnglish side reasons. (???) If you have requests or comments to the instructor, it is possible to tell ithem in advance.

7 - Accept tutor’s Skype request

Before the lesson starts, your instructor will send you a Skype request. Please approve this or you will not be able to contact each other on Skype! Now you’re ready for your lesson.

8 - After your conversation lesson, find your tutor’s review

When your instructor has assessed your lesson performance, you’ll see it at the top of your dashboard page. Cli ck the View feedback button to see more details.

9 - Continue on to the self-learning lessons.

You must find these lessons in Practical English 6 and study them yourself. These lessons will review and apply the expressions you have learned in your online con versation.

10 - Take the Review lesson

In the self-study course, take the Follow-up lesson (see numbers 2 and 3 above for help on finding the lesson). The follow-up lesson is a chance for you to review and use the language you learned in your online conversation.
